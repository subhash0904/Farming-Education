Black Root Rot: Rosellina arcuata

Symptoms:

The fungus originates from the dead heaped leaves of 5 – 7.5 above the soil level.

When bark is removed star-like growth of mycelium can be seen.

At the surface of the soil, the mycelium surrounds the stem and kills the bark for the length of 7.5 – 10.0 cm.

A swollen ring of tissue is formed round the stem above the dead patch.

Management:

  • Removal and destruction of infected plants.
  • Clean cultivation without fallen leaves.
  • Dig a trench around the infected bush to provide sunlight in the trench, which prevents the spread of mycelium.
Effective management practices involve prompt removal of infected plants, maintaining clean cultivation practices, and creating a trench to limit the spread of Black Root Rot caused by Rosellina arcuata in tea plantations.
