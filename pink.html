Managing Pink Disease in Crops: A Comprehensive Guide

Symptoms:

  • Prevalent in wet areas. Young twigs and branches are mostly affected.
  • The fungal growth encircles the stem, penetrates the bark and cortical tissues which eventually decay.
  • The bark splits and peels off. If the infection is not recognized early, the tree dies after the rainy season.

Management:

  • Affected parts should be pasted with Bordeaux paste or painting tar.
  • Copper fungicides should not be used in rubber because they will contaminate the latex.
By implementing these comprehensive management strategies, you can effectively control and mitigate the impact of Pink Disease in your crops. Regular monitoring, early detection, and timely intervention are essential for maintaining a healthy and productive crop.
