Disease Symptoms and Management Guide

Disease Symptoms:

Symptoms appear as small water-soaked spots with a sunken center on leaves.

Spots become white with a brown margin.

Lesions occur also on midribs, petioles, and lateral veins causing distortion and ragged appearance.

Lesions on the stem weaken the stem.

Survival and Spread:

  • Primary infection occurs by sowing infected seeds.
  • Secondary infection occurs by wind.

Favourable Conditions:

Rain and high humidity are favorable for the development of the disease.

By implementing these comprehensive management strategies, you can effectively control and mitigate the impact of the disease. Regular monitoring, early detection, and timely intervention are essential for maintaining a healthy and productive crop.
