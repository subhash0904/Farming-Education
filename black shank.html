Disease Symptoms and Management Guide

Disease Symptoms:

The disease is characterized by scattered, rapidly enlarging, irregular, brown, water-soaked lesions with characteristic gray-green borders.

Symptom development occurs particularly during and immediately following periods of heavy rains and high relative humidity.

Survival and Spread:

  • Primary infection occurs by inoculum present in plant debris in soil.
  • Secondary infection occurs by means of sporangia through air and water.

Favourable Conditions:

High soil moisture and moist weather conditions with intermittent showers are responsible for the development of the disease.

By implementing these comprehensive management strategies, you can effectively control and mitigate the impact of the disease. Regular monitoring, early detection, and timely intervention are essential for maintaining a healthy and productive crop.
