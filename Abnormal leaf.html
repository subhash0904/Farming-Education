Managing Abnormal Leaf Fall in Crops: A Comprehensive Guide

Symptoms:

  • It occurs during June - August, while general leaf fall occurs during December.
  • On leaves dull grey, circular spots appear which enlarge and become irregular.
  • The petiole exhibits a sunken spot. Affected fruits rot.
  • Leaves shed prematurely either green or after turning coppery red.
  • The affected leaf forms a thick carpet of rotting foliage which emits a bad smell.

Management:

  • Prophylactic sprays prior to the onset of the southwest monsoon with Bordeaux mixture 1%.
  • Addition of ZnSo4 @ 0.2% improves the efficacy.
By implementing these comprehensive management strategies, you can effectively control and mitigate the impact of Abnormal Leaf Fall in your crops. Regular monitoring, early detection, and timely intervention are essential for maintaining a healthy and productive crop.
